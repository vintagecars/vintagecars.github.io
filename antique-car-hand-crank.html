The hand crank was once a staple of automobiles, providing the power to turn the engine over and get the car started. Today, antique cars often feature a hand crank, with some cars even coming with a built-in crank. The hand crank has been around since the early days of the car, and has been used in many different types of vehicles. In this guide, we will take a look at the history of the antique car hand crank and discuss why it is still a popular choice for antique car collectors today.

History of the Antique Car Hand Crank

The hand crank was first used in cars in the early 1900s. It was a simple, yet effective way to start the engine and get a car moving. The hand crank was originally connected to a flywheel which was then connected to a starter motor. The flywheel provided the necessary torque to turn the engine over and start the car. As the years went on, the hand crank evolved and became more efficient. Some cars even had an automatic hand crank, which allowed the driver to start the car without having to manually turn the crank.

As cars became more sophisticated, the hand crank was eventually phased out and replaced by the electric starter in the 1920s. Despite this, the hand crank remained a popular choice for antique car collectors and enthusiasts. The hand crank has a certain charm and appeal that the electric starter can’t match. Plus, it is easy to use and doesn’t require a lot of maintenance.

Why Collectors Still Love the Antique Car Hand Crank

The antique car hand crank is still a popular choice for many antique car collectors. This is because it has a unique look and feel that the electric starter can’t match. Plus, it is easy to use and doesn’t require a lot of maintenance. The hand crank also adds a certain authenticity to the car, making it look and feel like an original antique. Plus, it is a great conversation starter when showing off your car at car shows.

The hand crank is also a great way to start your antique car. It is relatively simple to use and doesn’t require any special tools or knowledge. Plus, it can be a great way to get a feel for how the car was originally started. This can be especially useful for those who are restoring their cars to their original condition.

Types of Antique Car Hand Cranks

There are many different types of antique car hand cranks. Some of the most popular types include the traditional hand crank, the automatic hand crank, and the rotary crank. Each type has its own unique advantages and disadvantages, so it is important to research each type before deciding which one is right for your car.

The traditional hand crank is the most common type of hand crank. It is simple to use and doesn’t require a lot of maintenance. However, it can be difficult to turn the engine over if the crank is not in the correct position. The automatic hand crank is a bit more complicated, but it eliminates the need to manually crank the engine over.

The rotary crank is the most advanced type of hand crank. It is a bit more expensive than the other types, but it is also the most effective. The rotary crank can turn the engine over quickly and with minimal effort. It is also less likely to jam or break due to its design.

Where to Find Antique Car Hand Cranks

Finding an antique car hand crank can be a bit of a challenge. Many antique car parts stores carry them, but they may not have the specific type you are looking for. You can also try searching online for a specific type of hand crank. There are many online stores that specialize in antique car parts, and they may have the hand crank you are looking for.

Maintenance and Care of Antique Car Hand Cranks

It is important to properly maintain and care for antique car hand cranks. You should regularly check the crank for any signs of wear and tear, and replace any broken or damaged parts. You should also lubricate the crank regularly to ensure that it operates smoothly. Finally, you should store the crank in a cool, dry place to prevent rust or corrosion.

Conclusion

The antique car hand crank is a classic piece of car history. It has a unique charm and can add a certain level of authenticity to any antique car. Plus, it is easy to use and requires minimal maintenance. For antique car collectors and enthusiasts, the hand crank is a great addition to any car. It is a great way to get a feel for how the car was originally started, and it can be a great conversation starter when showing off your car at car shows.
