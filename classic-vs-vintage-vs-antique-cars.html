Classic, Vintage And Antique Cars: What's The Difference?

The terms classic, vintage and antique are often used interchangeably when referring to cars. However, there are distinct differences between them. Whether you're a classic car enthusiast, or just curious about the differences between these terms, this article will provide an overview of the terminology and explain how each type of car is defined.

Classic Cars

Classic cars come from a bygone era, usually dating from the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The exact definition of a classic car varies depending on the locality and the source, but most generally, classic cars are those that are at least 25 years old and retain the original characteristics of the model, meaning they have not been substantially modified. Classic cars are typically considered to be collectible vehicles, and they often require extensive maintenance and repairs.

Vintage Cars

Vintage cars, like classic cars, come from a certain era, but they also reflect a certain style. Vintage cars are typically between 20 and 40 years old and, unlike classic cars, they are usually modified, either for performance or aesthetic reasons. Vintage cars are often seen as stylish and desirable, and they can be used for both recreational and competitive purposes. They are usually more affordable than classic cars and require less maintenance.

Antique Cars

Antique cars are usually more than 40 years old, and they are often considered to be rare or valuable. These cars are usually considered to be "investment vehicles" and are rarely driven, as they tend to be fragile and require a great deal of maintenance and upkeep. As such, they are most often kept in showrooms or museums and cared for by professional collectors. Antique cars are typically more expensive than classic or vintage cars.

The Bottom Line

While the terms classic, vintage and antique are sometimes used interchangeably, there are distinct differences between them. Classic cars come from a bygone era and are typically 25 years old or more, while vintage cars are usually between 20 and 40 years old and are often modified. Antique cars are usually more than 40 years old and are considered to be rare and valuable. Whether you're a classic car enthusiast, or just curious about the differences between these terms, understanding the terminology can help you to make an informed decision.
