What Is an Antique Car?

In the state of Arkansas, an antique car is defined as a vehicle that is at least 25 years old. The vehicle must also have an original factory-installed engine and be of a make and model that is no longer in production. The vehicle must be primarily used for exhibition, parades, and other similar activities. It cannot be used for regular transportation.

How Do I Get an Antique Car License?

In order to obtain an antique car license in the state of Arkansas, you will need to fill out an application and submit it to the Arkansas Department of Motor Vehicles. You will also need to provide proof of ownership of the vehicle, such as a bill of sale or title. In addition, you will need to submit photographs of the vehicle, and a copy of a valid proof of insurance.

Are There any Restrictions on the Use of Antique Cars in Arkansas?

Yes, there are restrictions on the use of antique cars in the state of Arkansas.
