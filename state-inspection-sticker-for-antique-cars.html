What is a State Inspection Sticker For Antique Cars?

A state inspection sticker for antique cars is an official sticker issued by the government to verify that the vehicle meets state safety and emissions standards. The sticker is usually placed on the windshield of the vehicle, and is valid for one year. It is a legal requirement in most states for all vehicles to have a valid state inspection sticker.

What Are the Requirements For an Antique Car Inspection?

The requirements for an antique car inspection vary by state. Generally, the vehicle must pass a safety inspection and a smog check. The safety inspection will check the vehicle's brakes, lights, turn signals, and other safety features. The smog check will look for emissions levels that meet the standards set by the state. The antique car must also have the correct paperwork, including proof of ownership and registration.

What Benefits Does a State Inspection Sticker Provide?
