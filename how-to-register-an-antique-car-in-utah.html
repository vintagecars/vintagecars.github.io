Understanding the Rules and Requirements

According to the Utah Department of Motor Vehicles, antique cars must be at least 25 years old to be eligible for registration. This means that any vehicle manufactured before the year 1996 is eligible for antique registration. Additionally, antique registration in Utah is only available to vehicles that are solely used for exhibition and not for transportation. This means that the vehicle cannot be used for daily transportation. Furthermore, the vehicle cannot be registered for highway use and cannot be driven on public highways.

Submitting the Application

Paying the Fees

Once your application is approved and your antique registration certificate has been issued, you will need to pay the applicable fees. In Utah, the fee for antique vehicle registration is $25.00. This fee must be paid in full at the time of registration.

Renewing the Registration

In Utah, antique vehicle registration is valid for one year. Before the expiration date, you will need to renew your registration. To do so, you will need to submit a renewal application and pay the applicable fees. The renewal fee for antique vehicle registration in Utah is $15.00.

Important Regulations

When registering an antique vehicle in Utah, it is important to keep in mind that the vehicle must be kept in its original condition. This means that any modifications or alterations to the vehicle must be approved by the DMV before they can be made. Additionally, the vehicle cannot be used for transportation and cannot be driven on public highways.
