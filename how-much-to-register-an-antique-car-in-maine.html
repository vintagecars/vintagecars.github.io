How Much Does It Cost To Register An Antique Car In Maine?

Are you looking to register an antique car in Maine? If so, you will be pleased to know that the process is relatively straightforward. The state of Maine has specific rules and regulations that must be followed when registering an antique vehicle. In this article, we will discuss the cost of registering an antique car in Maine and the steps you will need to take to complete the registration process.

What Is an Antique Vehicle?

An antique vehicle is defined as any motor vehicle that was manufactured before 1946. This includes cars, trucks, vans, and motorcycles. The state of Maine requires that all antique vehicles be registered with the Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV). The registration process is different from that of a regular vehicle, as antique vehicles are not issued titles.

What Are the Requirements for Registering an Antique Vehicle in Maine?

In order to register an antique vehicle in Maine, you must provide proof of ownership. This could include a bill of sale, title, or registration from the previous owner. You will also need to complete and submit an application for antique vehicle registration. This form can be obtained from the BMV or online. You may also be required to provide a photograph of the vehicle.

How Much Does it Cost to Register an Antique Vehicle in Maine?

The cost of registering an antique vehicle in Maine depends on the type of vehicle you are registering. For cars, trucks, and vans, the registration fee is $35. For motorcycles, the registration fee is $25. You will also need to pay a one-time title fee of $20. In addition to the registration and title fees, you may also need to pay a sales tax of 5.5%, based on the vehicle’s purchase price.

What Other Fees Might I Need to Pay?

In addition to the registration and title fees, you may also need to pay a sales tax of 5.5%, based on the vehicle’s purchase price. You may also need to pay a processing fee of $2.50, as well as a fee for any specialty plates you may wish to add. Depending on the type of vehicle you are registering, you may also need to pay a one-time fee for a road test or emission inspection.

What Is the Process for Registering an Antique Vehicle in Maine?

Once you have gathered the required documents, you will need to visit your nearest BMV office. At the office, you will complete an application for antique vehicle registration. The application will require you to provide personal information such as your name, address, and date of birth. You will also need to provide information about the vehicle, such as the year, make, and model. Once you have completed the application, you will need to pay the registration and title fees.

What Documents Will I Receive After Registering an Antique Vehicle?

Once you have registered your antique vehicle, you will receive a Certificate of Registration. This document will serve as proof of ownership for your vehicle. You will also receive a set of license plates that you must affix to the vehicle. Additionally, you may receive a road test waiver or an emission inspection waiver, depending on the type of vehicle you are registering.

Conclusion

Registering an antique vehicle in Maine is a relatively simple process. The cost of registering an antique vehicle will vary depending on the type of vehicle you are registering. You will need to provide proof of ownership, complete an application for antique vehicle registration, and pay the registration and title fees. Once you have completed the registration process, you will receive a Certificate of Registration and a set of license plates.
