Introduction

Are you an antique car owner in Mississippi? If you are, you probably have some questions about the rules and regulations regarding antique cars in the state. Whether you use your vintage car for daily transportation or just for the occasional joyride, understanding the laws and regulations governing them is important in ensuring you are able to enjoy your vehicle safely and legally. This article will provide an overview of the Mississippi antique car rules and regulations for 2023.

Definition of an Antique Car in Mississippi

In Mississippi, an antique car is defined as a motor vehicle that is at least 25 years old and that has been maintained in such a manner that it is in the same condition as when it was initially purchased. This means that the vehicle must be in the same condition as when it was manufactured and must be free of any modifications, alterations, or additions. It should also be free of any rust or damage to the body and frame.

Registration and Titling Requirements

In order to legally drive an antique car in Mississippi, it must be properly registered and titled. In order to register and title an antique car, the owner must provide proof of ownership as well as proof of insurance. The owner must also pay any applicable registration and titling fees. Once the registration and titling process is complete, the owner will receive a special antique vehicle plate, which must be displayed on the vehicle at all times.

Inspection Requirements

In Mississippi, antique cars are exempt from state emissions and safety inspections. However, it is important to note that some counties may require an annual inspection of the vehicle in order to ensure it is in good working order. Additionally, it is important to remember that all drivers must adhere to the state's traffic laws and regulations, regardless of the vehicle type.

Insurance Requirements

Antique car owners in Mississippi are required to carry liability insurance on their vehicle. The minimum requirements for liability insurance in the state are $25,000 per person and $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, as well as $25,000 for property damage. It is important to note that some insurance companies may offer additional coverage options for antique cars.

Use of Antique Cars

In Mississippi, antique cars can be used for daily transportation, provided that the vehicle is properly registered and titled. However, the vehicle must only be driven between the hours of sunrise and sunset, unless the owner has obtained a special permit from the state. Additionally, the vehicle must not be used for any commercial purposes, such as delivering goods or providing transportation services.

Conclusion

Antique car owners in Mississippi must adhere to certain rules and regulations in order to keep their vehicles legal and safe. These rules include registration and titling requirements, as well as insurance and inspection requirements. Additionally, antique cars can only be used for daily transportation between the hours of sunrise and sunset, unless the owner has obtained a special permit from the state. By understanding and following the Mississippi antique car rules and regulations, vintage car owners can ensure they remain in compliance with the law and can enjoy their antique cars for years to come.
