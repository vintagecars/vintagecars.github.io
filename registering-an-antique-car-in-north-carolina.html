Registering An Antique Car In North Carolina

If you’re a resident of North Carolina who owns an antique car, you can take advantage of the many benefits offered by registering your vehicle as an antique. Not only will you be able to enjoy cheaper insurance premiums and other benefits, but you can also rest assured that your car is in good hands and compliant with the law. So, if you’re wondering how to register an antique car in North Carolina, read on for a few helpful tips.

Determine Eligibility

Before you can register your antique car in North Carolina, you’ll need to determine if your car is eligible. Generally, cars must be at least 25 years old to qualify as an antique. The car must also retain its original parts, with the exception of tires and the battery. Additionally, the car must be in good working order, with no substantial alterations to its engine or chassis.

Gather Required Documentation

Once you’ve determined that your car is eligible for registration as an antique, you’ll need to gather the necessary paperwork. First, you’ll need to bring proof of ownership, such as a title or registration from the current state. You’ll also need to bring proof of North Carolina residency, such as a driver’s license, utility bill, or tax return. Finally, you’ll need to bring proof of insurance for the car.

Complete the Application

The next step is to complete the application for registration. This application can be found online or in a local DMV office. You’ll need to provide your personal information, as well as information about the car, including the make, model, year, and VIN. You’ll also need to pay a registration fee of $15. Once your application is complete, you’ll be able to submit it to the DMV.

Take the Inspection

Once you’ve submitted your application and paid the registration fee, the next step is to take the inspection. Antique cars must pass an inspection in order to be registered. The inspection will include a visual inspection of the car’s body and chassis, as well as an emissions test. The inspector will also verify that the car is in good working order and that all the necessary paperwork has been submitted.

Receive Your Registration Certificate

Once your car passes the inspection, you’ll be able to receive your registration certificate and license plates. The registration certificate will need to be kept in the car at all times, and the license plates must be displayed on the car. The registration certificate will be valid for one year and will need to be renewed annually.

Renew Your Registration

Each year, you’ll need to renew your antique car registration. You can do this online or in person at a local DMV office. You’ll need to pay the renewal fee of $15 and submit proof of insurance. You’ll also need to take the car in for an annual inspection to make sure it’s still in good working order.

Enjoy the Benefits of Registration

Once you’ve completed the registration process, you’ll be able to enjoy the many benefits of having an antique car in North Carolina. You’ll be able to enjoy cheaper insurance premiums, as well as the satisfaction of knowing that your car is in good hands and compliant with the law.

Conclusion

Registering an antique car in North Carolina is a relatively straightforward process. All you need to do is determine eligibility, gather the required documents, complete the application, take the inspection, and receive your registration certificate. Once you’ve done this, you’ll be able to enjoy the many benefits of owning an antique car in North Carolina.
