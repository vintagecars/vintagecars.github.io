Antique cars are special vehicles that are usually more than 25 years old and have historic value. Many car enthusiasts around the world collect and restore these classic cars. It is important to note that antique cars are usually exempt from regular motor vehicle requirements like inspections. However, this may not always be the case, depending on the laws in the area.

What Is an Antique Car?

An antique car is defined as a vehicle that is more than 25 years old and has been well-maintained. These cars are usually not used for transportation purposes but rather kept as collector's items. Classic cars, on the other hand, are those that are more than 25 years old but are used for transportation.

Do Antique Cars Have to Be Inspected?

In most areas, antique cars are exempt from regular motor vehicle requirements like inspections. However, this may not always be the case, depending on the laws in the area. Some states require antique cars to be inspected and have the necessary paperwork in order in order to be driven on public roads.

Inspection Laws by State

In general, each state has its own laws regarding antique car inspections. For example, in California, antique cars do not need to be inspected. In contrast, in Illinois, antique cars must pass a safety inspection every two years. Similarly, in New York, antique cars must have a valid safety inspection certificate in order to be driven on public roads. It is important to check the requirements for antique car inspections with the local DMV before driving a classic car.

Inspection Exemptions

In most states, antique cars are exempt from regular inspections. However, there are certain exceptions. For example, in some states, antique cars may be exempt from emissions testing, but still be required to pass a safety inspection. Additionally, if an antique car is modified in any way, it may be subject to the same requirements as other vehicles.

Are Antique Cars Insured?

Antique cars are usually insured with a special type of insurance known as classic car insurance. Classic car insurance is designed specifically for antique cars and provides coverage for losses due to theft, fire, and other accidents. It is important to note that classic car insurance policies may have different requirements than regular car insurance policies, so it is important to read the policy carefully before purchasing.

Conclusion

Antique cars are usually exempt from regular motor vehicle requirements like inspections. However, this may not always be the case, depending on the laws in the area. It is important to consult the local DMV to find out if an antique car needs to be inspected before it can be driven on public roads. Additionally, it is important to purchase classic car insurance for antique vehicles in order to ensure proper coverage for losses due to theft, fire, and other accidents.
