Do you have an antique car you plan on driving around the Virginia area? If so, you should be aware of the laws and regulations that come with driving an antique car in Virginia. To ensure your safety on the roads and to be certain that you are in compliance with the state laws, it is essential that you pass an antique car inspection in Virginia before you get behind the wheel.

What is an Antique Car Inspection?

An antique car inspection in Virginia is a thorough assessment of your vehicle to ensure that it is safe to operate on the roads. The Virginia Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) requires that all antique cars be inspected before they can be legally driven in the state. The inspection includes a thorough examination of the car's interior and exterior, as well as its mechanical and electrical components. Once the inspection is complete, the inspector will provide you with a report of their findings.

Who Can Perform an Antique Car Inspection?

The Virginia DMV requires that all antique car inspections be performed by a licensed inspector. An inspector must be certified by the Virginia Department of Motor Vehicles in order to perform an antique car inspection. You can find a list of certified inspectors on the Virginia DMV website or contact your local DMV office for a list of approved inspectors in your area.

What Will the Inspector Look For?

When performing an antique car inspection, the inspector will look for any signs of damage or wear and tear in the car's interior and exterior. They will also inspect the mechanical and electrical components to make sure that they are functioning properly. The inspector will also check the vehicle's tires and brakes to ensure that they are in good condition. The inspector will then provide you with a report of their findings.

What Do I Need to Bring to the Inspection?

In order to complete an antique car inspection in Virginia, you must bring the following items to the inspection: your driver's license, proof of insurance, and a copy of the vehicle's registration. You should also bring any documents that you have related to the car, such as purchase receipts or repair records. Finally, you should bring any tools that you may need to assist the inspector, such as a flashlight or a tire pressure gauge.

How Much Does an Antique Car Inspection Cost?

The cost of an antique car inspection in Virginia varies depending on the type of vehicle being inspected and the inspector's fee. Generally, the inspection fee ranges from $50-$100. The cost of the inspection may also include additional fees for any additional services that the inspector may need to perform, such as a smog test or a road test.

When Do I Need to Get an Antique Car Inspection?

In Virginia, antique cars must be inspected every two years. You should plan to have your car inspected at least two months before the expiration date on your registration. This will give you enough time to make any necessary repairs or adjustments to your car before the inspection.
