Early Years

In the early years of law enforcement in the city of Fort Worth, the police force relied on horses for transportation. The horses were often used to patrol the streets and to transport officers to and from crime scenes. Horses were also used to transport prisoners to and from the city jail. During the late 19th century, the police force began to slowly transition away from horse-drawn vehicles and towards motorized vehicles.

Motorized Vehicles

By the early 20th century, the police force in Fort Worth had adopted motorized vehicles as their primary means of transportation. The first motorized vehicles used by the police force were cars and motorcycles. The cars and motorcycles were used to patrol the streets and to transport officers to and from crime scenes. The police force also used cars and motorcycles to transport prisoners to and from the city jail.

Antique Cars

In the early 20th century, the police force began to purchase antique cars for their use. The cars were usually vintage models that had been restored to their original condition. The antique cars were mainly used for ceremonial duties and for promotional events. The cars were also used to transport important dignitaries and VIPs. The antique cars are still owned by the Fort Worth Police Department today and are still used for ceremonial and promotional purposes.

Modern Vehicles

Today, the Fort Worth Police Department utilizes a variety of modern vehicles for their operations. The majority of the vehicles in their fleet are cars, SUVs, and trucks. The cars and SUVs are mainly used for patrol and transportation duties, while the trucks are mainly used for towing and equipment transport.
