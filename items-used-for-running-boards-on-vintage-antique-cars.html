What are Running Boards?

Running boards are long strips of metal or wood that are attached along the sides of a vintage antique car. They are also known as running boards, running strips, side steps, or sill plates. They were originally designed to make it easier to get into and out of the automobile. This is especially true of antique cars, as they typically have doors that are very low, making it difficult to climb in and out of the car. In addition to making it easier to enter and exit the car, running boards also provide a measure of protection from the elements and can help provide added stability for passengers as they enter and exit the vehicle.

Types of Materials Used for Running Boards

Running boards can be made of a variety of materials including wood, metal, and plastic. Wood running boards are the most traditional and are often found on vintage antique cars. They are usually made of oak, walnut, or mahogany and are polished to a high shine. Metal running boards are typically made of stainless steel and are resistant to corrosion and wear. Plastic running boards are also available but are less common due to their lack of durability.

Installation of Running Boards

The installation of running boards is typically a fairly straightforward process. The running boards can be either bolted or riveted onto the car. Many antique cars have pre-drilled holes in the body to make installation easier. If not, the car owner may need to drill the necessary holes themselves. It is important to ensure that the running boards are securely fastened to the car to avoid any potential damage to the body of the car.

Maintenance of Running Boards

Running boards require regular cleaning and maintenance in order to keep them in good condition. Wood running boards need to be sanded and waxed on a regular basis in order to prevent them from becoming too dry and brittle. Metal running boards need to be washed and waxed in order to prevent rust and corrosion. Plastic running boards can also be washed and waxed but are generally more resistant to damage and wear.

Replacement of Running Boards

If a running board becomes damaged or worn, it will need to be replaced. In the case of wood running boards, they can often be repaired by sanding and refinishing them. If the running board is beyond repair, it will need to be replaced. Metal and plastic running boards can also be replaced, but they will need to be of the same type and size of the original running board.

Costs of Running Boards

The cost of running boards can vary significantly depending on the material and size. Wood running boards are typically the most expensive, followed by metal and then plastic. The cost of the running boards will also depend on the size and complexity of the installation. It is important to shop around and compare prices before making a purchase.

Conclusion

Running boards are an important part of vintage antique cars. They provide a measure of protection from the elements and can make entering and exiting the car much easier. They come in a variety of materials including wood, metal, and plastic and can be attached to the car either by bolting or riveting. Regular cleaning and maintenance is necessary to keep the running boards in good condition and they will need to be replaced if they become too worn or damaged. The cost of running boards will vary depending on the material and size.
