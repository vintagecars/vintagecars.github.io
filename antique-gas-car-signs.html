Types of Antique Gas Car Signs

The most common type of antique gas car signs are the porcelain signs. These signs were made of a high-fired porcelain coated with enamel, and they were found at most gas stations in the early part of the 20th century. They are usually brightly colored and often feature some type of graphic or logo. They can be found in various sizes and shapes, from the common circle and rectangle to smaller shapes such as hearts, stars, and more.

The second type of antique gas car signs are the metal or tin signs. These signs were used in the early part of the 20th century and were made of a thin sheet of metal that had been painted with enamel. They are usually heavier and have a more solid look than the porcelain signs. They are usually more expensive, but they can be found in larger sizes and more elaborate designs.

The third type of antique gas car signs are the neon signs. These signs were used in the late part of the 20th century and feature bright colors and often feature some type of graphic or logo. They are usually quite expensive, but they can be found in larger sizes and more elaborate designs.
