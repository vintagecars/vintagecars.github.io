How Long Does It Take To Get An Antique Car Title In Pennsylvania?

If you have an antique car in Pennsylvania, you may be wondering how long it takes to obtain the title. This can vary depending on the situation, so there is no one-size-fits-all answer. It is important to know the process and what you need to do to make sure you get your title as quickly as possible.

What Is an Antique Car?

In Pennsylvania, an antique car is defined as any vehicle that is at least 25 years old. The age is determined by the model year, not the year it was manufactured. For example, a vehicle that was manufactured in 1995 but has a model year of 1994 would be considered an antique car in Pennsylvania.

Documents Needed for an Antique Car Title

To obtain an antique car title in Pennsylvania, you must first submit several documents to the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT). These documents include proof of ownership, a completed application for title, a bill of sale, and proof of Pennsylvania insurance. If you are transferring the title from another state, you must also submit proof of registration from the other state.

How Long Does It Take to Get an Antique Car Title?

Once the correct documents are submitted to PennDOT, the process can take anywhere from two weeks to six weeks, depending on the situation. If the documents are complete and there are no issues, the process should take less than two weeks. If there are issues, such as a lien on the vehicle, it can take up to six weeks to obtain the title.

Tips for Speeding Up the Process

To speed up the process of obtaining an antique car title in Pennsylvania, it is important to make sure all the documents are complete and accurate. You should double check the information on the application and make sure it matches the information on the other documents. If you are transferring a title from another state, make sure you have the proper paperwork from that state.

Where to Get an Antique Car Title

To apply for an antique car title in Pennsylvania, you must fill out the application for title and submit it to PennDOT. The application can be found on the PennDOT website and it must be submitted in person or by mail. You can also apply in person at your local PennDOT office.

How to Pay for the Title

Once the application is submitted, you must pay the title fee. This fee varies depending on the situation, so it is important to check with PennDOT for the exact amount. The fee can be paid by cash, check, or money order. If you are paying by mail, you must include a self-addressed, stamped envelope.

Conclusion

Getting an antique car title in Pennsylvania can take up to six weeks, depending on the situation. To make sure you get your title as quickly as possible, make sure all the documents are complete and accurate, and you have paid the title fee. If you follow these steps, you should be able to get your title in no time.
