Pennsylvania has a special process for those looking to register a car as an antique. Those who take the time to do so can benefit from some special advantages that come with having an antique car registered in the state. This article will explain the steps to register a car as an antique in Pennsylvania, as well as the advantages of doing so.

What is an Antique Car?

In Pennsylvania, a car is considered an antique if it is over 25 years old. Any car model produced prior to 1995 is eligible for antique status. To be considered an antique, the vehicle must also be used for occasional pleasure driving and not for regular transportation.

Steps to Register an Antique Car in Pennsylvania

The first step to registering a car as an antique in Pennsylvania is to provide proof of ownership. This can be done by submitting the vehicle's original title or a copy of the title that has been signed over to the current owner. Additionally, a bill of sale, if available, should be presented as well.

Once the ownership of the vehicle has been established, the vehicle must be inspected by the Pennsylvania State Police. This inspection is necessary to confirm that the vehicle meets the standards of an antique car as defined by the state.

After the vehicle passes inspection, the owner must submit the appropriate paperwork, along with the necessary fees, to the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation. This paperwork can be obtained from the state's Vehicle Services Department or online.

Advantages of Registering an Antique Car in Pennsylvania

The primary advantage of registering an antique car in Pennsylvania is that the vehicle is exempt from vehicle safety and emissions inspections. This means that the owner of an antique vehicle does not have to worry about the expense of maintaining it to pass these inspections.

Additionally, antique vehicles are exempt from certain taxes, such as sales and use taxes, as well as luxury taxes. This can result in significant savings for the owner of an antique vehicle.

Finally, antique vehicles are exempt from motor vehicle registration fees. This means that the owner of an antique car will not have to pay the annual registration fee that is required for regular vehicles.

Conclusion

Registering a car as an antique in Pennsylvania can bring significant benefits for the owner. The process is relatively simple and straightforward and can save the owner considerable time and money. Those looking to take advantage of these benefits should take the time to register their car as an antique in Pennsylvania.
