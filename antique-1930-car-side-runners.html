It's 2023 and cars have come a long way from their 1930 counterparts. However, there is something special about the cars of this era that still stands out and is appreciated by car enthusiasts and collectors alike. In this article, we are going to take a look back at vintage 1930 car side runners and how they helped to shape the cars of today.

What Are Side Runners?

Car side runners, also known as running boards, were a common feature on many cars of the 1930s. They were a platform that extended from the side of the car, typically running along the entire length of the car, and were designed to give the driver and passengers a safe place to stand when entering and exiting the car. Side runners also provided a platform for carrying luggage and other items.

The History of Side Runners

The side runners of the 1930s were an important part of the development of cars. They were first introduced in the 1920s, but it wasnâ€™t until the 1930s that they became more commonplace. At this time, cars were becoming larger and more powerful, and side runners provided a safe and secure way for passengers to get in and out of the car.

Due to their popularity, side runners were made available in a wide range of materials, including wood, metal, rubber, and even leather. This allowed car owners to customize their car and make it unique. By the end of the 1930s, side runners had become a standard feature on most cars.

How Side Runners Have Evolved

Today, side runners are no longer a standard feature on cars. Instead, they have been replaced by other features such as running boards, door sills, and mud flaps. While these features provide similar benefits to side runners, they are not as aesthetically pleasing or as practical. As a result, many car enthusiasts choose to install vintage 1930 car side runners when restoring their vehicles.

Benefits of Vintage Side Runners

Vintage 1930 car side runners offer a number of benefits. First, they provide a safe and secure way for passengers to enter and exit the car. Furthermore, they provide a stylish and unique look and feel to the car, which can add to its aesthetic appeal. Lastly, they can help to protect the car from dirt, mud, and other debris.

Where to Find Vintage Side Runners

If you are looking to install vintage 1930 car side runners on your car, you can find them at a number of different places. Many car restoration shops carry them, as do some online retailers. Prices can range from a few hundred dollars to a few thousand dollars, depending on the material and condition of the side runners.

Conclusion

Vintage 1930 car side runners are a great way to add a unique and stylish look to your car. They provide a safe and secure way for passengers to enter and exit the car, while also protecting it from dirt, mud, and other debris.
